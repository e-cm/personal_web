LIQUID LIGHT
2017

During the 2017 winter semester at Concordia, I was part of the Media Design sub-team of the larger Team Montreal, comprised of students from McGill University and Concordia University, for the Solar Decathlon China 2018 design competition. The Solar Decathlon is an international competition where teams of universities design modern homes that are economical and energy efficient.

The Media Design is tasked with designing interactive elements of the house that communicate with the habitants about their consumption habitats (electricity, water, etc.) in intuitive and aesthetically beautiful ways. During my time with them, I focused on a project tentatively titled Liquid Lamp.

Liquid Lamp is a light fixture that communicates water consumption and eventually electricity consumption data of the homeâ€™s habitants through liquid textures created in the lamp. My work throughout the semester focused on prototyping and programming. With help and input from other team members, I built a water pumping and dripping system, and programmed in Arduino and Max to create an interface for controlling the water and light to create different textures.
