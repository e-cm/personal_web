FLEECE LIGHTING
2018

I developed a responsive lights show that Montreal-based band Fleece used on their 2018 summer tour. A combination of stage design, industrial design, and software design, the lights aims to augment the bandsâ€™ live musical performances through dazzling visuals that respond to their music, while importantly being easy to setup, take down, and reconfigure.

The band's microphones and instruments feed into the program running the lights, causing colour and intensity shifts relative to the intensity of the music. New patterns are randomly generated throughout performances by use of a MIDI footpad.

The displays are composed of strips of LEDs. They allowed me to create an arrangement that is easier for transportation and setup because it can all fit into a duffel bag. For live shows, moving away from a simple screen helps achieve a more immersive spectacle by intertwining the band and the lights, allowing the visuals to mix more seamlessly with the music rather than just accompany it. The modularity of both the physical structure and the software allows for new experiences to be created every show, for both audience and performer alike.
