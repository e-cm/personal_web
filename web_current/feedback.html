FRACTAL FEEDBACK
1

  I'm fascinated by fractals, mandalas, and live visuals. Fractal Feedback is the result of an experiment with video feedback loops, created using Jitter within Max, and driven by my fractal obsession. I was inspired to try this out by discussions of feedback looping happening on the Cycling '74 forums.

2

  Video feedback has been a favorite of video artists since the conception of the medium, largely because it is simple to execute, yet results in gorgeous and ornate visuals. All you need to experiement with video feedback is a camera and a video feed of what the camera sees. Point the camera at its own feed and you begin to see an infinitely deep tunnel created by this loop, similar to when two mirrors are facing each other. When you begin to shift and rotate the camera is when things start getting wild.

3

  This patch operates on a similar principle: take a video source, rotate/zoom/colour it, blend the result with the current video feed, and repeat. Because of this endless loop of feeding a video an altered version of itself, changes in colour or form grow, flow over, and consume the structure. This gives the visuals a natural and spontaneous feel, yet still symetric and calculated.
